Step-by-Step Tutorial: Calculating Percentage Difference

  1. Identify the Initial Value and the Final Value: Determine the initial value (the starting point) and the final value (the ending point) for which you want to calculate the percentage difference. For example, let's say the initial value is 100 and the final value is 150.
  2. Calculate the Difference: Subtract the initial value from the final value to find the numerical change between the two numbers. In our example, subtracting 100 from 150 gives us a difference of 50.
  3. Calculate the Relative Difference: Take the absolute value of the difference calculated in the previous step. This ensures that the percentage difference is always positive, regardless of whether the final value is greater or smaller than the initial value. In our example, the absolute value of 50 is also 50.
  4. Calculate the Percentage Change: Divide the relative difference by the initial value. Then, multiply the result by 100 to obtain the percentage difference. In our example, dividing 50 by 100 and multiplying by 100 gives us a percentage difference of 50%.
  5. Interpret the Result: The percentage difference represents the relative change between the initial and final values, expressed as a percentage. A positive percentage indicates an increase, while a negative percentage indicates a decrease. In our example, a 50% percentage difference implies a 50% increase from the initial value of 100 to the final value of 150.
